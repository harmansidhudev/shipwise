# Product Design

> Phase 1: DESIGN | Sprint 5 (planned)

Guides product scoping, design methodology, and design system foundations.

## Coverage

- MVP scoping (MoSCoW, RICE scoring with calibration examples)
- User story format with acceptance criteria (Given/When/Then)
- Roadmap template (MVP → V1.1 → V2 with explicit out-of-scope)
- Pricing model design (tiers, trials, feature gates)
- Information architecture and user flow mapping
- Wireframe methodology (low-fi → mid-fi → high-fi)
- State inventory (empty, loading, populated, error, offline per screen)
- Design system foundation (color tokens, typography scale, spacing, components)
- Design handoff checklist
- Responsive strategy (breakpoints, mobile-first, touch targets ≥44px)
- WCAG 2.2 AA checklist (30 items at design stage)

## Checklist Items

### MVP Scope Definition
<!-- beginner -->
**Define your MVP scope** — List every feature you want, then ruthlessly cut. Use MoSCoW: Must-have (launch blocker), Should-have (soon after), Could-have (nice), Won't-have (out of scope). Your MVP should have 3-5 must-haves maximum.

<!-- intermediate -->
**MVP scope (MoSCoW + RICE)** — Must/Should/Could/Won't with RICE scoring for prioritization.

<!-- senior -->
**MVP scope** — MoSCoW + RICE scored.
