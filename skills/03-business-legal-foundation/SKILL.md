# Business & Legal Foundation

> Phase 1: DESIGN | Sprint 5 (planned)
> Disclaimer: Organizational checklists, not legal advice. Consult qualified professionals.

## Coverage

- Entity decision tree (LLC vs C-Corp x bootstrapped vs funded x US vs international)
- EIN application, business bank account (Mercury/Relay/Brex comparison)
- Accounting setup (QuickBooks/Xero/Wave, SaaS chart of accounts)
- Co-founder agreement (equity split, 4yr/1yr cliff vesting, roles, IP, departure)
- IP assignment for all contributors
- Domain portfolio (.com + variations, typosquat monitoring)
- Trademark (USPTO TESS search, Class 9/42 filing)
- Business insurance (GL, E&O, cyber liability, D&O)

## Checklist Items

### Entity Formation
<!-- beginner -->
**Choose and form your business entity** — If you're in the US: LLC is simpler and cheaper for bootstrapped projects. C-Corp is required if you want VC funding. Either way, you need an EIN (free from IRS) and a business bank account (Mercury is popular for startups).

<!-- intermediate -->
**Entity formation** — LLC for bootstrapped, C-Corp (Delaware) for funded. EIN + business banking.

<!-- senior -->
**Entity** — LLC/C-Corp + EIN + business banking.
