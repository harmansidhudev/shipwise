# Competitive Analysis Framework

## When to use

Use this framework after completing user interviews and before finalizing your positioning. A thorough competitive analysis answers three questions: (1) What already exists? (2) Where are the gaps? (3) How will you position yourself differently? This framework works for any product category — SaaS, marketplace, consumer app, or developer tool.

## Decision framework

```
What type of competitor is this?
│
├── Solves the SAME problem for the SAME audience?
│   └── Direct competitor — study closely, find gaps
│
├── Solves a RELATED problem, or same problem for DIFFERENT audience?
│   └── Indirect competitor — monitor, learn from their model
│
├── Not a product at all? (spreadsheets, manual process, hiring someone)
│   └── Alternative solution — this is often your real competition
│
└── Not sure?
    └── Ask: "Would my target user consider using this INSTEAD of my product?"
        ├── Yes → Direct or indirect competitor
        └── No → Not a real competitor (ignore it)
```

## How to gather competitive intelligence

### Free sources
1. **G2.com** — Filter reviews by company size and industry. Read 1-star and 2-star reviews for pain points.
2. **Capterra** — Similar to G2 but often has different reviewers. Compare both.
3. **Product Hunt** — Search for competitor launches. Read comments for first impressions and feature requests.
4. **Reddit** — Search "[competitor name] alternative" or "[competitor name] review" in relevant subreddits.
5. **App store reviews** — For mobile products, 1-3 star reviews are a goldmine of unmet needs.
6. **LinkedIn** — Check competitor employee count over time. Rapid hiring = growing; layoffs = struggling.
7. **BuiltWith / Wappalyzer** — See what technology competitors use (useful for understanding their technical stack and costs).
8. **SimilarWeb** (free tier) — Estimate competitor traffic, traffic sources, and geography.
9. **Competitor changelogs / blogs** — Subscribe to see what they are shipping and their strategic direction.

### Paid sources (optional, more relevant at growth stage)
- **Crunchbase** — Funding data, investor information
- **SimilarWeb Pro** — Detailed traffic and engagement metrics
- **SEMrush / Ahrefs** — Competitor SEO and content strategy

## Customization notes

- **Early-stage (pre-revenue)**: Focus on review sentiment analysis and feature gaps. You do not need a full moat assessment yet — that matters more at Series A.
- **Crowded market**: If there are 10+ direct competitors, group them into tiers (leaders, challengers, niche players) and deeply analyze only the top 3 plus the most similar niche player.
- **New market category**: If no direct competitors exist, your real competition is alternative solutions (spreadsheets, manual processes). Focus your analysis there.
- **B2B SaaS**: Pay extra attention to pricing models and procurement process. Enterprise buyers care about compliance, SSO, and audit logs — check if competitors have these.
- **Consumer products**: Emphasize review sentiment and app store ratings. Consumer switching costs are low, so speed and UX matter more than feature depth.
