# Lean Canvas Template

## When to use

Use this template at the start of idea validation, before user interviews. Fill it in with your best guesses, then update it as you gather real data. The Lean Canvas is a one-page business model that forces you to articulate every critical assumption in your startup — so you can systematically test each one.

---

## The 9 blocks explained

### 1. Problem (Top 3 problems)
What are the top 3 problems your target customers face? Be specific — "it's hard to manage projects" is too vague. "Freelance designers lose 5+ hours/week chasing invoice payments" is specific enough to test.

### 2. Customer Segments (Target users)
Who has these problems? Define your early adopter — the most desperate segment, not the broadest market. Early adopters are easier to find, more willing to try imperfect solutions, and give better feedback.

### 3. Unique Value Proposition (Single clear message)
One sentence that explains why your solution is different AND worth paying attention to. Formula: "[End result] for [target customer] unlike [alternatives]." This goes on your landing page headline.

### 4. Solution (Top 3 features)
The simplest possible features that address your top 3 problems. Resist listing more than 3 — if you need more, your problem definition is too broad. Each feature should map directly to a problem.

### 5. Channels (Path to customers)
How will you reach your early adopters? List the specific channels: subreddit names, Slack communities, newsletters, conferences, SEO keywords. "Social media" is not a channel — "r/freelanceDesigners" is.

### 6. Revenue Streams (How you make money)
Your pricing model: subscription (monthly/annual), usage-based, one-time purchase, freemium + premium. Include your target price point and why you believe customers will pay that amount.

### 7. Cost Structure (Fixed and variable costs)
What does it cost to build and run this? Include: hosting, third-party services (auth, email, payments), your time (opportunity cost), marketing spend. The goal: understand your burn rate and break-even point.

### 8. Key Metrics (Numbers that matter)
The 3-5 numbers that tell you if your business is working. For SaaS: MRR, churn rate, activation rate, CAC, LTV. Pick the ones that matter most at your stage — pre-launch metrics are different from growth metrics.

### 9. Unfair Advantage (What can't be easily copied)
Something that gives you a lasting edge: domain expertise, existing audience, proprietary data, network effects, personal brand, patents. If you don't have one yet, leave it blank — but start building one.

## Copy-paste template

```markdown
# Lean Canvas — [CUSTOMIZE] Product Name

Date: [CUSTOMIZE] YYYY-MM-DD
Version: [CUSTOMIZE] 1 (update as assumptions change)

## PROBLEM
1. [CUSTOMIZE] Top problem
2. [CUSTOMIZE] Second problem
3. [CUSTOMIZE] Third problem

**Existing alternatives:**
- [CUSTOMIZE] How do people solve this today?

## CUSTOMER SEGMENTS
**Early adopter:**
- [CUSTOMIZE] Who is the most desperate for a solution?

**Broader market (later):**
- [CUSTOMIZE] Who else has this problem?

## UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION
[CUSTOMIZE] Single clear sentence: why you, why now.

**High-level concept:**
- [CUSTOMIZE] "X for Y" analogy (e.g., "Uber for dog walking")

## SOLUTION
1. [CUSTOMIZE] Feature that solves Problem 1
2. [CUSTOMIZE] Feature that solves Problem 2
3. [CUSTOMIZE] Feature that solves Problem 3

## CHANNELS
- [CUSTOMIZE] Channel 1 (e.g., r/subredditName)
- [CUSTOMIZE] Channel 2 (e.g., Indie Hackers)
- [CUSTOMIZE] Channel 3 (e.g., Google search: "keyword")

## REVENUE STREAMS
- [CUSTOMIZE] Pricing model (e.g., $29/mo Pro, $99/mo Team)
- [CUSTOMIZE] Revenue target: $X MRR by month Y

## COST STRUCTURE
- [CUSTOMIZE] Hosting: $X/mo
- [CUSTOMIZE] Services (auth, email, payments): $X/mo
- [CUSTOMIZE] Marketing: $X/mo
- [CUSTOMIZE] Your time: X hours/week

## KEY METRICS
1. [CUSTOMIZE] Metric 1 (e.g., MRR)
2. [CUSTOMIZE] Metric 2 (e.g., Weekly active users)
3. [CUSTOMIZE] Metric 3 (e.g., Churn rate)

## UNFAIR ADVANTAGE
- [CUSTOMIZE] What do you have that can't be easily copied or bought?
  (Leave blank if none yet — but plan to build one)
```

## How to use this with Shipwise

1. **Fill the canvas** before user interviews (Skill 01 — Problem Definition + Persona)
2. **Update after interviews** — replace assumptions with evidence
3. **Update after landing page test** — validate Channels and UVP
4. **Update after concierge MVP** — validate Revenue Streams and Cost Structure
5. **Use in Go/No-Go decision** — a canvas full of validated assumptions = strong go signal

The Lean Canvas is a living document. Version it. Assumptions that survive testing become your business strategy. Assumptions that fail become pivots.
