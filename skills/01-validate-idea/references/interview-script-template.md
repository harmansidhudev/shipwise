# User Interview Script Template

## When to use

Use this script when conducting customer discovery interviews during idea validation. The script is designed around The Mom Test principle: ask about the interviewee's life, not your idea. These questions surface real behavior, real pain, and real spending — the only reliable predictors of whether someone will pay for your product.

Run this script with 5+ potential users before writing any code. Recruit interviewees from the channels identified in your persona (specific communities, LinkedIn outreach, social media groups).

## Decision framework

```
Should I interview this person?
├── Do they match my target persona?
│   ├── Yes → Proceed with interview
│   └── No → Skip (you will get misleading data)
│
├── Have I already interviewed 5+ people?
│   ├── Yes, and responses are converging → You have enough signal
│   ├── Yes, but responses are all over the map → Do 5 more, persona may be too broad
│   └── No → Keep going
│
└── Am I tempted to describe my solution during the interview?
    └── Stop. Ask about their life, not your idea.
```

### The interview script (20 questions)

```markdown
# User Interview Script
# Domain: [CUSTOMIZE — e.g., "expense tracking for freelancers"]
# Interviewer: [CUSTOMIZE]
# Date: [CUSTOMIZE]

---

## Phase 1: Warm-Up (3 questions, ~3 minutes)
## Goal: Build rapport, understand context

1. "Can you tell me a bit about your role and what a typical
   day/week looks like for you?"
   [CUSTOMIZE — adapt "role" to your domain. For consumers,
   ask about their routine related to the problem area.]

2. "How long have you been doing [CUSTOMIZE — their role or
   relevant activity]?"

3. "What are the biggest challenges you deal with regularly
   in [CUSTOMIZE — the domain area]?"

---

## Phase 2: Current Workflow (5 questions, ~10 minutes)
## Goal: Understand how they solve the problem today

4. "Walk me through how you currently handle
   [CUSTOMIZE — the specific process your product addresses].
   Step by step, from start to finish."

5. "How often do you go through this process?
   Daily? Weekly? Monthly?"

6. "How long does it typically take you each time?"

7. "What tools or methods do you use right now for this?"
   [Listen for: spreadsheets, manual processes, competitor
   products, workarounds, delegation to others]

8. "Is anyone else involved in this process?
   Who, and what do they do?"

---

## Phase 3: Pain Points (4 questions, ~8 minutes)
## Goal: Identify what hurts and how much

9. "What is the most frustrating part of how you
   handle this today?"
   [Listen for emotional language: "I hate," "drives me crazy,"
   "waste of time"]

10. "Can you tell me about the last time this problem
    really caused you trouble? What happened?"
    [Specific stories > general complaints. Press for details.]

11. "When this goes wrong, what does it cost you?
    Time? Money? Missed opportunities? Stress?"
    [Try to get a concrete number or estimate]

12. "On a scale of 1-10, how painful is this problem
    for you right now? Why that number and not higher/lower?"

---

## Phase 4: Solutions Tried (3 questions, ~5 minutes)
## Goal: Understand what they have already tried and why it failed

13. "Have you tried to solve this problem before?
    What did you try?"

14. "What worked and what did not work about those
    solutions?"

15. "Why did you stop using [solution they mentioned],
    or why are you still putting up with the current way?"
    [Key insight: reasons people abandon solutions reveal
    what they truly value]

---

## Phase 5: Willingness to Pay (3 questions, ~5 minutes)
## Goal: Determine if they would actually spend money

16. "Are you currently paying for anything to help with
    this? What, and how much?"
    [Current spending = strongest signal. If they pay $0
    today, converting them to paid is much harder.]

17. "If something could [CUSTOMIZE — describe the outcome,
    NOT your product features] and save you
    [CUSTOMIZE — the time/money cost from Q11],
    what would that be worth to you per month/year?"
    [Anchor to the cost of the problem, not to your price]

18. "What would make you say 'shut up and take my money'
    for a solution to this problem? What is the minimum
    it would need to do?"

---

## Phase 6: Wrap-Up (2 questions, ~2 minutes)
## Goal: Open-ended insight and referral

19. "Is there anything about [CUSTOMIZE — the problem domain]
    that I should have asked about but did not?"

20. "Do you know 2-3 other people who deal with this same
    problem that I could talk to?"
    [Referrals are gold — warm intros convert much better
    than cold outreach]

---

## After the interview:
- Thank them and send the incentive within 24 hours
- Fill out the Post-Interview Synthesis (below) immediately
  while the conversation is fresh
```

### Interviewer guidelines

```markdown
# Interviewer Guidelines — Read Before Every Interview

## The Mom Test Rules
1. Talk about THEIR life, not YOUR idea
2. Ask about the PAST and PRESENT, not hypothetical futures
3. Talk less, listen more (aim for 80/20 — they talk 80%)
4. Never say your product name or describe features
5. Get specifics: "Can you tell me more about that?"
   beats "Interesting."

## Red Flags — Answers to Be Skeptical Of
- "Yeah, I would totally use that!" → Polite fiction.
   Ask instead: "When was the last time you tried to solve this?"
- "That is a great idea!" → Compliment, not data.
   Ask instead: "How much do you currently spend on this?"
- "I think people would love that" → Opinion about others.
   Ask instead: "What do YOU do right now?"
- Future promises ("I would pay $X") → Unreliable.
   Past behavior ("I already pay $X for Y") → Reliable.

## Green Flags — Answers That Indicate Real Pain
- Specific stories with emotional detail
- Money already being spent on partial solutions
- Time estimates that reveal significant waste
- Unsolicited requests: "When can I get this?"
- Referrals without being asked
- They lean forward / speak faster / show visible frustration

## Logistics
- Schedule 30 minutes (most finish in 20-25)
- Video call or in-person; avoid text/email interviews
- Record with permission (ask at the start)
- Have a note-taker if possible so you can focus on listening
- Send a calendar invite with clear topic: "Research conversation
  about [CUSTOMIZE — domain, e.g., expense tracking]"
```

## Customization notes

- **B2B products**: Replace warm-up questions with company-specific context (team size, tech stack, approval process for new tools). Add a question about procurement: "Who decides on new tools, and what is the approval process?"
- **B2C products**: Shift demographic questions toward lifestyle and habits. Replace "role" with "daily routine" and "budget" with "what you spend on [category] per month."
- **Marketplace / two-sided products**: Run separate interview scripts for each side (e.g., buyers and sellers). Pain points and WTP will differ significantly.
- **Technical users**: You can go deeper on workflow specifics and tool integrations. Non-technical users need simpler language.
- **Remote interviews**: Use Zoom or Google Meet with recording. Tools like Grain, Otter.ai, or Fireflies.ai can auto-transcribe.

## Companion tools

None — this is a pre-code activity. Conduct interviews via video call or in person. Use any note-taking tool you prefer (Google Docs, Notion, pen and paper).
