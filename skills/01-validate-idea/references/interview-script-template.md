# User Interview Script Template

## When to use
Before building anything — validate the problem exists and is worth solving by talking to 5+ potential users.

## Decision framework
Use the Mom Test approach: ask about their life, not your idea. Never ask "Would you use X?" — ask "How do you currently handle Y?"

## Copy-paste template

```markdown
# User Interview Script — [Your Product Name]

## Pre-interview (2 min)
- Thank them for their time
- "I'm researching how people handle [problem area]. No right or wrong answers."
- "Can I record this? It helps me remember details."

## Current workflow (10 min)
1. Walk me through the last time you [did the thing your product replaces].
2. What tools/processes do you currently use for this?
3. How often do you do this? (daily/weekly/monthly)
4. What's the most frustrating part of your current approach?
5. How much time does this take you currently?

## Pain depth (5 min)
6. On a scale of 1-10, how painful is this problem?
7. Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?
8. Have you paid for any solutions? How much?
9. If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?

## Alternatives (3 min)
10. What other tools have you looked at?
11. Why didn't they work for you?
12. What would make you switch from your current approach?

## Willingness to pay (3 min)
13. If a tool solved [specific pain point], what would that be worth to you?
14. What's your budget for tools in this category?
15. Who makes purchasing decisions? (B2B only)

## Wrap-up (2 min)
16. Is there anything I didn't ask that I should have?
17. Do you know 2-3 other people who face this problem?
18. Can I follow up with you as we build this?

## Post-interview notes
- Key quotes:
- Pain level (1-10):
- Current spend on alternatives:
- Willing to pay:
- Referrals:
```

## Customization notes
- Adjust questions 13-15 based on B2C vs B2B audience
- For B2B: add questions about procurement process, team size, compliance requirements
- Record the interview (with permission) and transcribe — don't rely on notes alone
