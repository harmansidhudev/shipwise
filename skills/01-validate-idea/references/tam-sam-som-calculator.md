# TAM/SAM/SOM Calculator

## When to use
When estimating market size using bottom-up methodology (not top-down).

## Decision framework
Always use bottom-up. "The CRM market is $50B" is useless. "There are 2M freelance designers who spend $50/mo on invoicing tools" is actionable.

## Copy-paste template

```markdown
# Market Size — [Your Product]

## Bottom-up calculation

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
Everyone who could theoretically use your product.

- Number of [target users] globally: ___
- Average annual spend on this problem: $___
- **TAM = ___ x $___ = $___/year**

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
The segment you can actually reach with your go-to-market.

- Geographic filter (e.g., English-speaking): ___% of TAM
- Segment filter (e.g., teams of 5-50): ___% of TAM
- **SAM = TAM x ___% x ___% = $___/year**

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)
Realistic first-year capture.

- Realistic market share in year 1: ___% (typically 1-5%)
- **SOM = SAM x ___% = $___/year**

## Worked example: B2B SaaS for freelance invoicing
- TAM: 70M freelancers globally x $600/yr on invoicing = $42B
- SAM: 15M English-speaking x 40% use digital tools = $3.6B
- SOM: 0.5% market share year 1 = $18M
```

## Customization notes
- Use government data, industry reports, and LinkedIn for user counts
- Cross-validate with competitor revenue estimates
- Investors want to see bottom-up, not top-down
